# TIP-005: The Quantum Spectrum — Controlling Component Crystallization Radius

**Status:** Recommended for structured decomposition in AI Oriented Architecture.

## Context

One of the largest sources of Crystallization Radius is uncontrolled component growth.

As systems evolve, features accumulate inside increasingly large execution surfaces:

* services become feature containers;
* modules become dependency hubs;
* business rules become scattered across unrelated files.

For human developers this creates maintenance overhead.

For AI agents it creates reasoning overhead.

The agent must continuously determine:

* where a responsibility belongs;
* what can be modified safely;
* which dependencies matter;
* which components participate in a workflow.

We need a predictable decomposition model.

We call it the Quantum Spectrum.

---

## The Problem

Traditional architectures often rely on informal boundaries:

* components;
* services;
* modules;
* utilities;
* helpers.

These terms rarely define expected size, responsibility, or reuse patterns.

As a result:

* some services become oversized responsibility hubs;
* some utilities become hidden dependency hubs;
* some modules accumulate unrelated responsibilities.

The Crystallization Radius expands because component boundaries become ambiguous.

---

## The Quantum Spectrum

The Quantum Spectrum defines three levels of architectural granularity:

* Micro Actors
* Nano Actors
* Pico Actors

These are cognitive and architectural boundaries.

They are not deployment requirements.

A Micro Actor, Nano Actor, or Pico Actor may execute:

* inside a single application;
* inside a modular system;
* inside a distributed platform;
* inside a microservice ecosystem.

The purpose of the spectrum is reducing reasoning complexity, not prescribing deployment topology.

---

## Micro Actors

Micro Actors define primary business boundaries.

Examples:

* Billing
* Inventory
* Ordering
* Customer Management

Responsibilities:

* own business capabilities;
* define bounded contexts;
* contain local business workflows.

For AI agents:

Micro Actors provide the highest-level reasoning boundary.

A task should always begin here.

---

## Nano Actors

Nano Actors encapsulate reusable business workflows.

Examples:

* ApplyDiscount
* ValidatePromotion
* CalculateTax
* DetermineShippingMethod

Responsibilities:

* coordinate business rules;
* support reuse across features;
* prevent logic duplication.

For AI agents:

Nano Actors isolate business behavior into predictable modification units.

---

## Pico Actors

Pico Actors encapsulate deterministic execution primitives.

Examples:

* FindCustomerById
* FormatInvoiceNumber
* PublishTelemetryEvent
* SerializeContract

Responsibilities:

* perform one explicit operation;
* expose minimal ambiguity;
* contain little or no branching logic.

For AI agents:

Pico Actors provide highly localized reasoning surfaces with extremely small Crystallization Radius.

---

## The Extraction Rule

The Quantum Spectrum follows a simple principle:

**Start large. Extract downward only when reuse pressure appears.**

Workflow:

### Step 1

Implement new behavior inside the parent Micro Actor.

### Step 2

If business behavior becomes reusable, extract it into a Nano Actor.

### Step 3

If deterministic technical behavior becomes reusable, extract it into a Pico Actor.

This prevents premature abstraction while preserving future flexibility.

---

## Why It Matters

The Quantum Spectrum reduces Component Crystallization Radius.

Instead of asking:

> Where in the system does this behavior belong?

The agent operates within a predictable hierarchy.

Benefits:

* smaller reasoning surfaces;
* improved retrieval precision;
* reduced dependency traversal;
* improved code graph locality;
* safer autonomous modifications;
* reduced abstraction sprawl.

---

## Relationship to AI Oriented Architecture

The Quantum Spectrum exists to support Local Reasoning.

Every extraction should reduce the amount of context required to understand or modify behavior.

If an extraction increases Crystallization Radius, it should be reconsidered.

The goal is not more components.

The goal is better reasoning boundaries.

---

## Recommendations

For AI engineering teams:

* Start implementation inside the owning Micro Actor.
* Extract only under proven reuse pressure.
* Keep Nano Actors business-focused.
* Keep Pico Actors deterministic and explicit.
* Treat component boundaries as reasoning boundaries.
* Measure success by reduced Component Crystallization Radius.

Do not decompose software to create more units.

Decompose software to create better reasoning locality.

#### TIP-001: AI Usage as a KPI - The Anti-Pattern of 2026

Status: Widely adopted. Increasingly harmful.

### Context

Many companies, afraid of falling behind in the AI race, started adding AI usage metrics to employee KPIs:

* Number of prompts
* Tokens consumed
* Time spent in ChatGPT / Claude / Copilot
* AI session counts

These metrics quickly became tied to performance reviews, rankings, bonuses - and sometimes layoffs.

### What Happened

Employees adapted faster than management expected.

The result was widespread token burning:

* Launching dozens of unnecessary AI agents
* Rewriting the same request 15+ different ways
* Generating polished AI documents that nobody reads or uses

### Consequences

* The illusion of higher productivity
* Penalizing genuinely effective employees
* Declining quality of real work
* Growing cynicism and burnout
* Leadership gets impressive dashboards while losing actual business value

### Recommendations

For employers:

Measure outcomes, decision quality, customer impact, and execution speed - not AI usage itself.

AI is a tool, not the objective.

For employees:

Don�t optimize for token burn just to satisfy a KPI.

High-quality AI work usually means fewer prompts, not more.

Goodhart�s Law always catches up.

And when it does, things get uncomfortable.
